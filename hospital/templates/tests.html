Medical Tests

X-ray

An X-ray is a medical imaging test that helps physicians diagnose and treat medical conditions.

  • Uses radiation to produce images of internal structures
  • Often used to diagnose bone fractures and lung diseases
  • Typically takes only a few minutes to complete

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)

MRI is a non-invasive medical imaging test that produces detailed images of internal structures.

  • Uses a magnetic field and radio waves to produce images
  • Often used to diagnose soft tissue injuries and neurological conditions
  • Takes longer to complete than X-ray, typically 30-60 minutes

Ultrasound

An ultrasound is a non-invasive medical test that uses high-frequency sound waves to produce images of internal organs and tissues.

  • No radiation is involved
  • Often used to visualize the heart, blood vessels, and reproductive organs
  • Typically takes only a few minutes to complete

Computed Tomography (CT) Scan

A CT scan is a medical imaging test that uses X-rays and computer technology to produce detailed images of internal structures.

  • Uses more radiation than X-ray
  • Often used to diagnose cancer, trauma, and vascular disease
  • Typically takes 10-30 minutes to complete
